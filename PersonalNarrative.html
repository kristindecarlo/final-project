Personal Narrative

In doing my final website I feel like I really utilized everything that I learned in class. I made my final website into a project portfolio. I wanted to make it something that I could show friends, family, or even future employers when I am looking for an internship. The pages I made were a homepage, an about me page, a page with my resume, a contact information page, and a page Singapore experience page. In my about me page I wanted to just share a little bit about things and myself I like. The writing sample page and resume page are two pages I think would be relevant to future employers. My resume page has information about my past work experience and the writing sample page has links to my published writings. As I was making the pages I used a lot of skills I learned in class, such as coding, designing the pages, and bootstrap.

A lot of thought went into the style of my web page. I wanted to make a page that was fun and showed my personality, but was also clean, easy to read, and professional. My favorite colors are pink and purple, so I wanted to be sure to include these. One aspect I really like on my page is the header that is on top of every page. For this I used a background image and then placed my name over it. This background image is what decided the shades of pink and purple I used throughout my whole page since I included it on every page. I got the font I used from Google fonts and also incorporated that throughout my entire website. I used margins of 30px on every page as well. One thing that was really important to me was consistency throughout my website. I want the person looking at it to focus on the content and not getting distracted by changes in design or too many crazy things going on each page.

I used bootstrap in two different ways in my final website. The first way I customized bootstrap was by making a navigation bar. Once I got the template I just had to add different categories in it. I decided to keep the search box in my website since I see it on many other websites I go in. I ended up deleting a lot in the navigation bar because there were a lot more pages there that I did not need. The other way I used bootstrap was to put my pictures into my Singapore experience page. This was a bit harder for me, I ended up having to go on a website about css to learn how to do it. I further customized it by having a different amount of pictures in each section, so I often had to add parts of the grid. I also decided to center the grid because I thought that was what looked best. On my homepage I put pictures in, and I did not use bootstrap for this. This was extremely hard for me. I had to resize the pictures a bunch of times and float them and use a clear fix. When I used bootstrap for the pictures I was able to put them in a container and edit all of the sizes more easily, and I did not have to figure out the perfect margins, padding, or anything else. All I had to do was change the size of the pictures. Adding pictures in was the hardest part of this project, but with bootsrap it was a little bit easier.

One aspect of coding that helped me the most throughout this project was creating classes for my material. I used more than one class in most pages as a way to organize my information. Before this final project I really avoided classes because I thought they were complicated. But, I didn’t realize how much easier they make editing CSS and doing styling. If I hadn’t used classes on my final website my project would have been a disaster. One other thing that helped me throughout this project was using the validator. Normally I waited until I was done with the project to validate my work and see what was wrong, but this time I validated my work as I went. I think this saved me a lot of time and energy because each time I only had a few errors apposed to waiting until the end and having so many errors and not knowing what to do.

In the course of doing my final project, the project portfolio, I got to utilize all of the skills I learned in class. I organized my content and design in my site prototypes, I used the coding I learned, and used a lot of styling and css. Even though I had some trouble making this site, and this class was harder than I thought it would be, it was worth it and I made a site that I can be proud of.
